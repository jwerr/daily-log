# 2026-07-18

Topic focus: Fine-tuning vs prompt engineering

What I worked on: Compared fine-tuning against prompt engineering as two ways to adapt an LLM to a specific task, and when each one is the better tool.

What I learned: Prompt engineering (few-shot examples, system instructions, chain-of-thought) is fast, cheap, and reversible, and it's usually the right first move since it needs no training data or GPU cost. Fine-tuning updates the model's weights on labeled examples, which pays off when you need consistent formatting, a narrow domain vocabulary, or behavior that prompting can't reliably enforce - but it costs more, needs a curated dataset, and locks in a specific model version. In practice, teams usually push prompting as far as it goes (often combined with RAG) before reaching for fine-tuning.
