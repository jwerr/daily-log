Topic focus: RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation)

What I worked on: Studied how RAG pipelines are structured - the retrieval step (chunking documents, embedding them, and searching a vector store) and the generation step (feeding retrieved chunks into an LLM as context).

What I learned: RAG grounds an LLM's answers in real, retrievable documents instead of relying only on what it memorized during training. This cuts hallucination and lets the model answer questions about private or up-to-date data without needing to be fine-tuned. The quality of retrieval (chunk size, embedding model, similarity search) matters as much as the generation model itself.

References:
Retrieval-Augmented Generation for Knowledge-Intensive NLP Tasks (Lewis et al., 2020): https://arxiv.org/abs/2005.11401
Pinecone - What is RAG?: https://www.pinecone.io/learn/retrieval-augmented-generation/
