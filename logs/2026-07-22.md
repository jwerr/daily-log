# 2026-07-22

Topic focus: Model evaluation and bias

What I worked on: Looked at how LLMs get evaluated beyond accuracy - benchmarks like MMLU and HellaSwag, and fairness/bias evaluation across demographic groups and prompt framings.

What I learned: Benchmark scores alone can be misleading - a model can top a leaderboard yet fail badly on real-world edge cases or specific subgroups. Good evaluation combines automated benchmarks, held-out human evaluation, and targeted bias/fairness testing rather than relying on one aggregate number.

References:
Stanford HELM (Holistic Evaluation of Language Models): https://crfm.stanford.edu/helm/
